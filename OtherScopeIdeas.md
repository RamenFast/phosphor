A few other new scope concepts
Spectral Weather

A pressure-map metaphor rather than bars. Synchrosqueezed frequency ridges become wind lines; broadband noise becomes fog; tonal stability creates high-pressure cells; sudden spectral flux produces fronts and lightning. It would be excellent for ambient, noise, and orchestral material.

Phase Orchard

Each frequency band grows a branch determined by inter-channel phase. Stable mono content forms a trunk; wide correlated material creates orderly branches; decorrelated reverberation creates a fine canopy; polarity inversions visibly grow backward.

Rhythm Loom

Onsets are threads laid across a rotating loom. Repeating inter-onset intervals weave persistent motifs. Polyrhythms become overlapping fabrics rather than competing bar meters. Unlike a normal tempogram, it emphasizes the history of relationships between attacks.

Harmonic Cartography

The Tonnetz becomes a navigable topography. Harmonic motion leaves a trail across fifth, major-third, and minor-third terrain. Modulation literally moves the visual center of gravity; pedal tones become landmarks; chromatic instability roughens the ground.

Causal Constellation

Each salient sound event becomes a star. Similar timbres cluster; repeated motifs reconnect to earlier stars; bass events bend nearby paths; silence lets the constellation drift. Over a full track, the song builds a reproducible visual fingerprint.

Of these, Recurrence Bloom feels most naturally native to Phosphor. It keeps the immediate physical pleasure of a glowing beam, preserves actual scope information, and adds the one property most visualizers lack:

memory. 🌱📺
